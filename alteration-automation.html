Financial Alteration Automation

Home Alteration Automation

This project aims at automating the alteration of insurance policies to reduce manual intervention, maintain data integrity and improve efficiency across departments.

Process Automation

Automated the manual alteration process for insurance policies, which previously required approvals from multiple department heads (Operations, Actuarial, and IT).

Eliminated the need for manual database entries, significantly reducing the time and effort needed to perform alterations.

Data Analysis and Prioritization

Generated detailed reports from the database to identify the most frequent alterations performed on insurance policies.

Classified alterations into three categories: essential automations, necessary automations, and “nice to have” automations. Prioritized them based on the business value and frequency of occurrence.

Compliance with General Terms and Conditions

Researched and reviewed the general terms and conditions for each type of alteration and product to understand the criteria for when specific alterations were possible.

Ensured that these conditions were built into the automated system, preventing unauthorized alterations.

Cross-functional Collaboration

Worked closely with the operations team to identify required documentation for each alteration type.

Collaborated with the actuarial team to gather the necessary formulas for various insurance products and alterations.

Coordinated between departments to ensure smooth handoffs and accurate implementation of automated processes.

Development and Testing

Provided comprehensive instructions and requirements to the development team to implement the automated solution.

Conducted extensive testing post-implementation to ensure the automation performed as expected.

Oversaw user acceptance testing (UAT) and managed the production deployment of the automated alterations.

Testing and Quality Assurance

Developed comprehensive test plans and test cases to ensure data accuracy and system functionality.

Performed regression testing to verify that changes did not introduce new bugs.

Collaborated with stakeholders to conduct user acceptance testing (UAT) to ensure the system met their requirements.

Complex Approval Process

Simplified a multi-step approval process by automating parts that required approval from the Head of Operations, Head of Actuaries, and Head of IT, reducing the overall processing time.

Formula and Data Integration

Ensured accurate implementation of actuarial formulas and alteration data, coordinating between IT and actuarial teams to validate the logic and testing thoroughly before production deployment.

Outcome

The project significantly reduced the time and effort required for policy alterations, saving resources across IT and Actuarial teams.

Automated a large portion of the alterations process, reducing dependency on manual interventions and increasing operational efficiency.
